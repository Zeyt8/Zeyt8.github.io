Techtonic

Gameccelerator 2019

The game is a 3D 2-player LAN co-op puzzle/shooter where 2 players take control of a father and a drone that try to esacape the underground they are in.

The action takes place in an underground steampunk city. Suddenly the earth shakes, you pick up your weapon and together with your drone you go explore to see what happened.

The place is swarming with hostile soldiers and you need to fight them while solving puzzles to progress through the level. Most things you do require cooperation.

The father deals the damage, but the drone is small and can reach remote places. All your abilities interact with the enviroment, so don't hesitate to try. At last, your abilities also pair up to make unique effects.

At the end of the cave, right before you make your exit, you are greeted by a big robot. Let the boss fight begin!

You can download and play the game here: Techtonic

Notable mechanics

- The drone has an ability that pushes all things around it. This can be used to push the other player to higher places.

- The drone's harpoon has an eletrical charge. With it the drone can power up generators and control the rail switch.

- The father's jump can break certain objects if he has enough velocity on impact. Velocity can be aquired by falling from high places.

- The father's grenade, if it detonates near the drone's harpoon has an area of effect discharge that powers up nearby electrical devices.

- The vents are blocked by steam pipes and the father needs to switch them in order to help the drone pass

- The boss fight can only be won through cooperation. The boss gets staggered after a certain amount of hits. Then the players need to do 1 of the 2 combos. They can either use the grenade electrical discharge or get the father to a high places from where to fall upon the boss.

Development

The project was made for Gameccelerator 2019, a competition for an internship at Ubisoft Bucharest. I worked as the sole programmer.

For the networking part, I learned how to use Unity HLAPI, based on Remote Client Commands and Server/Master Client Commands.

The development took place for 4 months, full of work. I gained experience in building a 3d game, using networking APIs, IKs and making cinematics.
